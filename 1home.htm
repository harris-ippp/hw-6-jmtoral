A General Panorama of Drug Criminal Organizations (DCOs) in México

Organized Crime is the biggest challenge of public policy in Mexico today. DCOs do not provide only drugs, but they are involved in a diverse portafolio of illegal activities.

Moreover, the experts agree that the main bussiness of DCOs is the protection market. They provide a juridical order filling the space of ordinary legal institutions designed to enforce contracts.

In Mexico there are several DCOs and subsidiary armed groups. The following table present the most important and the territories in which they operate:

DCO Name Territories
Cártel Jalisco Nueva Generación Baja California, Nayarit, Zacatecas, Aguascalientes, Colima, Veracruz, Hidalgo, Michoacán, Guerrero, Estado de México, Oaxaca and Chiapas.
Cártel de Sinaloa Sinaloa, Baja California, Guerrero, Chihuahua, Coahuila, Durango.
Los Zetas Quintana Roo, Tabasco, Nuevo León, Tamaulipas, Coahuila.
Cartel del Golfo Tamapulipas, Quinatana Roo.
Caballeros Templarios Michoacán, Colima, Estado de México, Morelos.
